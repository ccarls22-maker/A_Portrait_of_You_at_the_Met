# About the App

Hi there! My name is Courtney, and throughout my education I've studied both Business Analytics and Art History. 
Now I'm out to combine the two in a fun, interactive way.

The Met has over 1 million works in its collection, making it difficult to ensure you get the most out of your visit. 
I designed this app to curate specific art pieces that fit your personality and mood.

While this GitHub-based app uses around 70,000 pieces of art for faster loading, you can download the Streamlit file 
and full Met CSV to curate your own collection of over 400,000 pieces locally. (The GitHub selection was reduced to save load times.)

# Step 1: Define general Periods

Let's Start by creating classifications for the art so we can begain to parse the "vibe" / tone for the art.

# Ancient Art:
Before 800 BCE (this is usually classified by geography)
(Topics: Sacred ritual, power of rulers, the afterlife, foundational storytelling in visual form.)

# Art Periods:
Greek/Roman: 800 BCE - 400 CE
(Topics: Human idealism (Greek), civic power & realism (Roman), mythology, perfected form.)

# Medieval: 400 - 1350
(Topics: The glory of God, religious narrative, hierarchy over realism, symbolism.)
# (Movements within: Byzantine, Romanesque, Gothic. ) # Note to self maybe break into geogrpahy for these? 

# Renaissance: 1350 - 1600
(Topics: Humanism, rediscovery of classical art, scientific perspective, individual genius.)
# (Movements within: Early Renaissance, High Renaissance, Mannerism.) # Note to self break by time

# Baroque: 1600 - 1750
(Topics: Drama, tension, grandeur, theatrical light, engaging the senses.)
# *(Movements within: Rococo [late Baroque, ~1720-1760])*

# Neoclassical: 1750 - 1800
(Topics: Civic virtue, logic, order, classical models from Ancient Greece/Rome.)

# Romantic: 1800 -1865
(Topics: Emotion, the sublime in nature, individualism, dramatic rebellion.)


# Art Movements:
Impressionism: 1865 - 1885
(Topics: Modern life, fleeting light, sensory impression, painting en plein air.)

# Post-Impressionism: 1885 - 1910
(Topics: Symbolic color, structural form, emotional and personal expression.)
(Related: Fauvism, Expressionism.)

# Modern: 1910 - 1960
(Topics: Experimentation, abstraction, rejecting tradition, art about art itself.)
(Key Movements: Cubism, Futurism, Dada, Surrealism, Abstract Expressionism.)

# Contemporary: 1960 - Now
(Topics: Conceptualism, identity politics, globalization, questioning boundaries.)
(Key Movements: Pop Art, Minimalism, Performance Art, Installation, Postmodernism.)

# Step 2: Define Categories for Viewing


Let's create some options for a viewers..

# Step 2.1: Personality Archetypes (Broad Filter)
These are the foundational lenses through which a user sees the world.
---

## The Classicist: 

Values order, balance, clarity, and timeless ideals. Seeks harmony and perfected form.

Potential Art Match: Greek/Roman, Renaissance, Neoclassical.


## The Romantic: 
Driven by emotion, intuition, and imagination. Drawn to the dramatic, the mysterious, and the sublime power of nature.

Potential Art Match: Romantic, Baroque, Gothic, some Surrealism.


## The Realist: Pragmatic, observant, and grounded. Values truth, authenticity, and the unvarnished moment.

Potential Art Match: Realism, Social Realism, some Dutch Golden Age.

## The Innovator: 
Experimental, abstract, and intellectual. Challenges conventions and explores ideas, form, and perception itself.

Potential Art Match: Cubism, Futurism, Abstract Expressionism, much of Modern art.

## The Symbolist: 
Seeks deeper meaning, metaphor, and the spiritual. Drawn to mystery, dreams, and coded narratives.

Potential Art Match: Medieval, Symbolist movement, Surrealism, much of Contemporary conceptual art.

## The Hedonist: Sensual, decorative, and attuned to pleasure, beauty, and the elegance of surface.

Potential Art Match: Rococo, Impressionism, some Post-Impressionism (like Renoir), Art Nouveau.

# Step 2.2: Moods / Vibes (The Specific Filter)
These are the immediate emotional or atmospheric states.
---
# A. Energy & Composition
## Dynamic & Dramatic: 
Diagonal lines, intense contrast, theatrical movement.
Periods: Baroque, Romantic, Futurism.
## Calm & Balanced: 
Horizontal lines, symmetrical composition, serene subjects.
Periods: Renaissance, Neoclassical, Minimalism.
## Chaotic & Raw: 
Energetic brushwork, fragmented forms, intense emotion.
Periods: Expressionism, Abstract Expressionism, some Contemporary.
---
# B. Color & Light
## Warm & Golden: 
Rich golds, deep reds, chiaroscuro (strong light/shadow).
Periods: Renaissance, Baroque, Romantic.
## Cool & Ethereal:
Icy blues, silvery light, misty atmospheres.
Periods: Gothic, Romantic (Caspar David Friedrich), some Surrealism.
## Vibrant & Unnatural: 
Bold, non-representational color used for emotional impact.
Periods: Fauvism, Post-Impressionism (Van Gogh), Pop Art.
## Muted & Naturalistic: 
Earth tones, "real" light, subtle gradations.
Periods: Realism, Impressionism (overcast days), Dutch Golden Age.
---
# C. Subject & Concept
## The Human Figure:
Idealized, expressive, or deconstructed.
Periods: Greek/Roman, Renaissance, Expressionism, Cubism.
## Nature & Landscape: 
Sublime, pastoral, or scientifically observed.
Periods: Romantic, Impressionism, Realism, Hudson River School.
## The Inner World:
Dreams, the subconscious, spiritual visions.
Periods: Surrealism, Symbolism, Medieval visionary art.
## Urban & Modern Life
The city, technology, leisure, social critique.
Periods: Impressionism, Realism, Ashcan School, Futurism, Pop Art.
## The Abstract Idea: 
Pure form, color, geometry, or conceptual premise.
Periods: Abstract Expressionism, Minimalism, Conceptual Art.
---
# D. Texture & Surface
## Smooth & Polished: 
Perfect marble, flawless glaze, invisible brushwork.
Periods: Greek/Roman, Renaissance, Neoclassical.
## Textured & Painterly
Visible brushstrokes, thick impasto, evidence of the artist's hand.
Periods: Impressionism, Post-Impressionism, Abstract Expressionism.
## Ornate & Decorative
Intricate detail, pattern, gilt, and curvature.
Periods: Rococo, Gothic, Art Nouveau.
